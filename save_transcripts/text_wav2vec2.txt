His abode, which he had fixed at a bowiery or country seat at a short distance from the city, just at what is now called Dutch Street, soon abounded with proofs of his ingenuity patent smoke jacks that required a horse to work them. Dutch ovens that roasted meat without fire carts that went before the horse's weather cocks that turned against the wind and other wrong-headed contrivances that atonished and confounded all beholders.
